Nelson Mandela: Long Walk to Freedom

Easy Summary

Nelson Mandela was a great leader of South Africa. He fought against apartheid, a system that treated black people unfairly. After many years of struggle and imprisonment, Mandela became the first black President of South Africa on 10 May 1994. In this chapter, Mandela talks about his inauguration ceremony in Pretoria. He remembers the sacrifices made by many freedom fighters. Mandela says that courage does not mean having no fear. It means overcoming fear. He also explains that every person has two obligations: one towards family and another towards society and country. According to Mandela, true freedom means living with dignity, equality and respect. The chapter teaches us the value of freedom, equality and human rights.

3–5 Marks Important Questions

Question 31 (5 Marks)

Describe the inauguration ceremony of Nelson Mandela.

Answer

The inauguration ceremony was held on 10 May 1994 in Pretoria, South Africa. Leaders from many countries attended the ceremony. Nelson Mandela took the oath as the first black President of South Africa. The ceremony marked the end of apartheid and the beginning of democracy. It was a historic and memorable day for the people of South Africa.

Question 32 (5 Marks)

What does Mandela say about courage?

Answer

Mandela says that courage does not mean the absence of fear. Everyone feels fear in difficult situations. A courageous person is one who overcomes fear and moves forward. According to Mandela, true courage is the victory over fear. This idea helped him during his struggle for freedom.

Question 33 (5 Marks)

What are the twin obligations of every person?

Answer

Mandela says that every person has two obligations. The first obligation is towards family, parents and children. The second obligation is towards society and the country. A person should fulfill both responsibilities honestly. These obligations help in building a better society.

Question 34 (4 Marks)

How did Mandela's idea of freedom change with age?

Answer

As a child, Mandela thought freedom meant doing whatever he wanted. As he grew older, he realized that freedom means having equal rights and dignity. He understood that many people in South Africa were not free. This inspired him to fight against injustice and discrimination.

Question 35 (5 Marks)

Why did Mandela fight against apartheid?

Answer

Mandela fought against apartheid because it was unfair and inhuman. Black people were denied basic rights and opportunities. He wanted equality, justice and freedom for everyone. He spent many years in prison during this struggle. His efforts finally helped bring democracy to South Africa.

Question 36 (4 Marks)

What kind of society did Mandela dream of?

Answer

Mandela dreamed of a society where all people were equal. He wanted a country free from discrimination and hatred. Everyone should have equal opportunities and respect. His dream was a peaceful and democratic South Africa.

Question 37 (5 Marks)

Describe the character of Nelson Mandela.

Answer

Nelson Mandela was brave, determined and kind-hearted. He fought for freedom and equality throughout his life. He never gave up despite facing many difficulties. He believed in justice and human dignity. His leadership and sacrifice made him a respected world leader.

Question 38 (4 Marks)

What lessons do we learn from Mandela's life?

We learn the importance of courage, determination and equality. Mandela teaches us to fight against injustice. He also teaches us to respect the rights of others. His life inspires us to work for a better society.

Question 39 (5 Marks)

What is apartheid and how did it affect South Africa?

Answer

Apartheid was a system of racial discrimination in South Africa. Black people were treated unfairly and denied equal rights. They faced social, political and economic discrimination. This system created suffering and division in society. Mandela and many others fought to end apartheid.

Question 40 (4 Marks)

Why was Mandela's inauguration a historic event?

Answer

Mandela's inauguration was historic because he became the first black President of South Africa. It marked the end of apartheid. The event symbolized freedom, equality and democracy. It gave hope to millions of people.

Question 41 (3 Marks)

Mandela respected the freedom fighters because they sacrificed their lives for justice. They fought bravely against discrimination. Their efforts helped South Africa achieve freedom.
