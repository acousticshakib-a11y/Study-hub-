The Hundred Dresses - I

Easy Summary

The story is about a poor girl named Wanda Petronski. She studied in a school in America. Wanda was quiet and simple. She always wore the same faded blue dress. Some girls in her class, especially Peggy and Maddie, made fun of her. They laughed when Wanda said that she had one hundred dresses at home. Although Wanda was poor, she was very talented in drawing. Later, she won the school drawing contest by submitting beautiful designs of one hundred dresses. After Wanda left the school, Peggy and Maddie realized their mistake. They felt sorry for making fun of her. The story teaches us kindness, respect and the importance of not judging others.

Wanda sat in the last row because her clothes were often muddy and untidy. She usually came to school wearing the same faded blue dress.

The girls made fun of Wanda because she claimed to have one hundred dresses. They did not believe her and thought she was telling lies.

Answer

Maddie felt guilty because she knew it was wrong to laugh at Wanda. However, she remained silent and did not stop Peggy.

Why did Wanda say that she had one hundred dresses?

Answer

Wanda said she had one hundred dresses because she dreamed of having many beautiful dresses. She later proved her imagination through her drawings.

What kind of girl was Peggy?

Answer

Peggy was intelligent and popular, but she often made fun of Wanda. She did not realize that her jokes were hurting Wanda.

Question 22 (2 Marks)

How was Maddie different from Peggy?

Answer

Maddie was kinder and more sensitive than Peggy. She felt sorry for Wanda and understood her pain.

What competition was announced in the school?

The school announced a drawing and colouring competition. Students were asked to submit their best artistic work.

Why was Wanda absent from school?

Wanda was absent because her family had moved away from the town due to the teasing and discrimination they faced.

Question 25 (2 Marks)

What did Wanda win in the competition?

Wanda won the girls' drawing competition by submitting one hundred beautiful dress designs.

How did Wanda prove that she was talented?

Wanda proved her talent through her excellent drawings. Her one hundred dress designs impressed everyone in the school.

The story teaches us to respect others, avoid bullying and treat everyone with kindness and understanding.

3 Mark Important Questions

Question 31 (3 Marks)

Describe the character of Wanda Petronski.

Answer

Wanda Petronski was a quiet, simple and hardworking girl. She never argued with her classmates even when they teased her. She was also very talented in drawing and showed great patience and dignity.

Question 32 (3 Marks)

Why did the girls tease Wanda?

Answer

The girls teased Wanda because she always wore the same blue dress and claimed to have one hundred dresses at home. They thought she was lying and made fun of her regularly.

Question 33 (3 Marks)

How did Maddie feel about Peggy's behaviour towards Wanda?

Answer

Maddie was uncomfortable with Peggy's behaviour. She knew that teasing Wanda was wrong, but she was afraid to speak against Peggy. This made her feel guilty.

Question 34 (3 Marks)

Why was Wanda absent from school for many days?

Answer

Wanda's family decided to move to another town. They were tired of being teased because of their unusual name and background. Therefore, Wanda stopped coming to school.

Question 35 (3 Marks)

What was Wanda's greatest talent?

Answer

Wanda's greatest talent was drawing. She created one hundred beautiful dress designs and won the school drawing competition.

Question 36 (3 Marks)

How did Wanda surprise everyone in the school?

Answer

Wanda surprised everyone by winning the drawing competition. Her one hundred dress designs were beautiful and creative, proving her exceptional artistic ability.

Question 37 (3 Marks)

Why did Maddie feel sorry after Wanda left?

Answer

Maddie realized that Wanda had been hurt by the teasing. She regretted not defending Wanda when she had the chance.

Question 38 (3 Marks)

What does the story teach about bullying?

Answer

The story teaches that bullying can deeply hurt others. We should always treat people with kindness and never make fun of them because of their appearance or background.

Question 39 (3 Marks)

What was Peggy's attitude towards Wanda?

Answer

Peggy often laughed at Wanda and asked questions to make fun of her. However, she did not fully understand how much Wanda was being hurt.

Question 40 (3 Marks)

Why is Wanda an inspiring character?

Answer

Wanda is inspiring because she remained calm and patient despite being teased. She proved her worth through her talent and hard work rather than through arguments.
