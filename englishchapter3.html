Two Stories About Flying

Easy Summary

This chapter contains two stories. The first story, "His First Flight", is about a young seagull who was afraid to fly. His parents encouraged him, but he lacked confidence. Finally, hunger forced him to jump and he discovered that he could fly. The second story, "The Black Aeroplane", is about a pilot who got lost in a storm at night. A mysterious black aeroplane appeared and guided him safely to the airport. Later, he found that no such plane was seen on the radar. Both stories teach us confidence, courage and hope.

Why was the young seagull afraid to fly?

Answer

He was afraid because he thought his wings would not support him.

Question 2 (1 Mark)

Who encouraged the young seagull to fly?

Answer

His parents encouraged him to fly.

Question 3 (1 Mark)

What forced the seagull to fly?

Answer

His hunger forced him to fly.

Question 4 (1 Mark)

What was the pilot flying?

Answer

He was flying an old Dakota aeroplane.

Question 5 (1 Mark)

What guided the pilot through the storm?

Answer

A mysterious black aeroplane guided him.

2 Marks Important Questions

Question 6 (2 Marks)

How did the young seagull finally start flying?

Answer

When his mother tempted him with food, he jumped towards it. Instead of falling, he started flying.

Question 7 (2 Marks)

Why was the seagull feeling lonely?

Answer

His brothers and sister had already learned to fly. He was left alone on the ledge.

Question 8 (2 Marks)

Why did the pilot enter the storm?

Answer

He wanted to reach home quickly and thought he could cross the storm safely.

Question 9 (2 Marks)

What problem did the pilot face inside the storm?

Answer

He lost his way and his instruments stopped working properly.

Question 10 (2 Marks)

Why was the pilot surprised after landing?

Answer

He learned that no other aeroplane was seen on the radar.

3 Marks Important Questions

Question 11 (3 Marks)

Describe the feelings of the young seagull before his first flight.

Answer

The young seagull was frightened and lacked confidence. He believed that his wings were too weak to support him. Therefore, he refused to fly.

Question 12 (3 Marks)

How did the seagull's family help him?

Answer

His family encouraged him to fly. They called him repeatedly and tried to motivate him. His mother finally used food to make him fly.

Question 13 (3 Marks)

Why was the pilot happy while flying?

Answer

The sky was clear and he was on his way home. He was excited to have breakfast with his family.

Question 14 (3 Marks)

What happened when the pilot entered the storm?

Answer

The clouds became dark and thick. He lost visibility and his instruments became unreliable.

Question 15 (3 Marks)

Why is the black aeroplane called mysterious?

Answer

The black aeroplane appeared suddenly and disappeared after helping the pilot. No one could explain its existence.

4 Marks Important Questions

Question 16 (4 Marks)

Describe the first flight of the young seagull.

Answer

The young seagull was very hungry. When he saw food with his mother, he jumped towards it. At first he was frightened, but soon he discovered that he could fly. He then flew confidently with his family.

Question 17 (4 Marks)

What lesson do we learn from 'His First Flight'?

Answer

The story teaches us self-confidence and courage. Fear can be overcome through effort and determination. Success comes when we believe in ourselves.

Question 18 (4 Marks)

Describe the storm faced by the pilot.

Answer

The storm was very dangerous. Thick black clouds surrounded the plane. The pilot lost direction and could not see anything clearly.

Question 19 (4 Marks)

How did the black aeroplane help the pilot?

Answer

The black aeroplane appeared in the storm and guided the pilot safely through the clouds. It helped him reach the airport safely.

Question 20 (4 Marks)

What is the main theme of 'The Black Aeroplane'?

Answer

The story highlights hope, mystery and faith. It shows that help may appear unexpectedly during difficult situations.

5 Marks Important Questions

Question 21 (5 Marks)

Describe the character of the young seagull.

Answer

The young seagull was timid and lacked confidence. He was afraid to fly because he thought his wings were weak. However, he finally overcame his fear and learned to fly. His success teaches us the importance of self-belief.

Question 22 (5 Marks)

How did the young seagull overcome his fear?

Answer

The seagull was hungry and wanted food. His mother tempted him with a piece of fish. He jumped towards it and discovered that he could fly. Thus, he overcame his fear through action.

Question 23 (5 Marks)

Describe the mystery of the black aeroplane.

Answer

The black aeroplane appeared during a dangerous storm and guided the pilot safely. After landing, the pilot found that no such plane was visible on the radar. This made the incident mysterious and unexplained.

Question 24 (5 Marks)

What lessons do we learn from both stories?

Answer

Both stories teach confidence, courage and hope. The seagull learns to trust himself, while the pilot receives unexpected help during a crisis. They inspire us to face challenges bravely.

Question 25 (5 Marks)

Why is 'Two Stories About Flying' an inspiring chapter?

Answer

The chapter inspires readers to overcome fear and remain hopeful. It shows that confidence leads to success and that help can come in unexpected ways during difficult times.
