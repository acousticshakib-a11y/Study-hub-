Glimpses of India

Easy Summary

Glimpses of India is divided into three parts. The first part, A Baker from Goa, describes the traditional baking culture of Goa. Bakers were an important part of Goan life. The second part, Coorg, describes the beauty of Coorg, a famous hill station in Karnataka. It is known for its natural beauty, coffee plantations and brave people. The third part, Tea from Assam, tells us about tea gardens in Assam. Rajvir and Pranjol travel through Assam and learn about the history and production of tea. The chapter introduces different cultures, traditions and natural beauty of India.

Coorg

Easy Summary

Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a beautiful district in Karnataka. It is famous for its coffee plantations, green hills and pleasant weather. The place is called the land of rolling hills. The people of Coorg are brave and hospitable. Many Coorgi people have served in the Indian Army. The river Kaveri originates from the hills of Coorg. Tourists visit Coorg to enjoy its natural beauty, wildlife and adventure sports.

Tea from Assam

Easy Summary

Tea from Assam tells the story of Rajvir and Pranjol's journey to Assam. Pranjol's father worked as a manager in a tea garden. During the journey, Rajvir saw vast tea plantations and beautiful scenery. He shared interesting facts about tea and its history. Assam is famous for producing some of the finest tea in the world. The chapter highlights the beauty of Assam and the importance of tea cultivation.
